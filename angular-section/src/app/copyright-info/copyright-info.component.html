DMCA Takedown Procedure

Last updated: Nov, 29, 2023

Gmanley ("us", "we", or "our") respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects our users to do the same. If you believe that your copyrighted work has been copied in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, please follow the procedure below to submit a DMCA takedown notice:

1. Identify the Infringing Material

1.1 Provide a clear and detailed description of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed.

1.2 Identify the specific location of the infringing material on our website, including URLs.

2. Provide Contact Information

2.1 Include your contact information, including your full name, address, email address, and phone number.

2.2 If you are acting on behalf of the copyright owner, provide your relationship to the copyright owner.

3. Include a Statement of Good Faith Belief

3.1 Include a statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

4. Include a Statement Under Penalty of Perjury

4.1 Include a statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

5. Provide Your Electronic Signature

5.1 Electronically sign your notice with your full legal name.

6. Send the Notice

6.1 Send your DMCA takedown notice to our designated agent at the following address:

Griffin Manley
Western University
gmanleyuwo.ca

7. Review and Removal

7.1 Once we receive your complete DMCA takedown notice, we will review it promptly and take appropriate action, including the removal of the infringing material if necessary.

Note: Misrepresentations made in your takedown notice may result in legal consequences. Please ensure that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner.
